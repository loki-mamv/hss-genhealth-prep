Why Ortho/Surgical PA Is Especially Painful

  • Nearly every procedure requires PA ‚Äî joint replacements, spine surgery, arthroscopy, imaging (MRI/CT), injections
  • Complex clinical documentation ‚Äî operative notes, imaging reports, conservative treatment history, BMI requirements
  • Multiple payer portals ‚Äî each insurer has different requirements, forms, and portals
  • Time-sensitive ‚Äî surgical scheduling depends on PA approval; delays = lost OR time and revenue
  • High denial rates ‚Äî ortho procedures face above-average initial denial rates
  • Appeals are resource-intensive ‚Äî peer-to-peer reviews pull surgeons from the OR

The Regulatory Tailwind

CMS-0057-F Rule (Jan 2026)

Requires payers to implement electronic PA APIs (FHIR-based) by Jan 2027. HSS will need partners who can connect to these new digital rails.

Gold-Carding Legislation

Several states pushing PA exemptions for high-performing providers. HSS's outcomes data positions them well ‚Äî but they need the tech infrastructure to take advantage.
